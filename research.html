Research Portfolio

Research at Meta (Reality Labs – VR)

At Meta, I led and collaborated on research within the VR space, examining how immersive environments shape user identity, presence, and emotional experience. My work included in-depth user studies, diary studies, and co-creative sessions to inform the design of social and embodied VR experiences. Key themes included digital selfhood, presence, and the ethics of identity design in virtual spaces.

Research at i2 Media Research Lab

At i2 Media (Goldsmiths, University of London), I worked on user-centered research exploring how people interact with emerging technologies and digital interfaces. Our focus was on accessibility, user trust, and meaningful engagement with digital products. Methods included mixed-method UX studies, qualitative interviews, and technology trials with diverse participants.
